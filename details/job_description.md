KPMG in Canada is scaling an internal team of AI Builders who operate as the engine embedded in the business—powering the firm’s agent builds and transforming our business

 

This team is for people who sit at the intersection of enterprise operations, technology, and design, and who can look at how the firm works today, imagine how it should operate with agentic AI, and make that future real through execution.

 

Our objective is to move beyond experimentation and enable real internal adoption of agentic AI, redesigning core workflows, decision systems, and operating models using autonomous agents that power KPMG’s service lines and internal teams. 

 

Within this role, AI Builders take ownership of the full build lifecycle, working closely with Engineering and Internal Transformation teams to move solutions from our KPMG AI Lab into scalable, reliable production systems, all within a complex and regulated enterprise.

 


What you will do

At the Senior Consultant level on this team, you will be a hands‑on AI Builder who takes ownership of well-scoped internal problems and contributes to building real, agentic solutions end to end. You will work closely with senior builders, practice leads, and internal stakeholders on rapid experimentation, prototyping, and Lab‑based initiatives that demonstrate and validate new agentic capabilities.

 

In this role, you will:

 

AI Builder:

Build, test, and harden agents, plugins, skills, and tools using the firm’s approved technology stack, working in deep connection with cloud and engineering teams to deliver production‑grade solutions.
Own the full build lifecycle: prototype in the AI Lab, productionize through Internal Transformation, and maintain solutions post‑deployment with support from engineering and platform teams.
Work with production systems, design evaluations, and handle integrations with enterprise platforms and data sources.
Contribute reusable skills, components, and tools back into the shared AI stack so each subsequent build starts further ahead.
Operate inside the shared builder pool, deployed against the highest‑leverage agent builds across the organization.
Be an active member of the AI Lab, contributing demos, reusable assets, and helping build a strong builder community across the firm.

What you bring to the role

At KPMG in Canada, we know the strongest builders come from a wide range of backgrounds, including engineering, business, strategy, design, and self‑taught technical paths. This role is designed for early‑career professionals,   who are ready to build, learn, and grow quickly in a complex and regulated enterprise environment.

You bring enterprise fluency and understand how organizations operate.
You can identify real pain points, constraints, and opportunities where agentic AI creates tangible value.
You think in workflows, not just models, designing multi‑step processes that account for human-AI handoffs, decision boundaries, and appropriate levels of autonomy.
You have a builder mindset and don’t just describe solutions, you create them, whether through code, configuration, low‑code platforms, APIs, or orchestration tools.
You take end‑to‑end ownership, from problem definition through to a working system that people actually use.

You can make reasonable assumptions, build quickly, test, learn, and iterate. You also apply sound judgment, treating risk, governance, ethics, and trust as core design constraints rather than afterthoughts.

What does the recruitment process look like?

 

Our recruitment process for these roles is designed to be practical, transparent, and focused on real skills and potential.

 

Step 1: Apply

Submit your application with your CV that includes a hyperlink to a prototype portfolio. This can include a link to a hosting platform with examples of AI solutions, tools, experiments, or projects that demonstrate how you approach problem-solving and build with AI.

 

Step 2: Shortlist and prototype case

Shortlisted candidates are invited to complete a prototype build case submission and participate in an interview. This stage mirrors real‑world work and gives you the chance to show how you think, design, and apply agentic AI in practice.

 

Step 3: Hiring decision

Final candidates move forward to a hiring decision, with a focus on skills, collaboration, and responsible, scalable impact.
