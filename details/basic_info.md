DETAILS

AI Builder Candidate Assignment Brief
Assessment exercise for KPMG AI Builder Senior Consultant and Manager candidates

Timebox: Maximum 3 hours total
Tools: Candidate's own tools, accounts, and AI tokens
Assessment-only assurance
This exercise is intended to evaluate your approach, judgment, and builder mindset. It is not
a request to produce a formal hiring asset for immediate or current-cycle use. Nothing
submitted as an assignment will be used by KPMG for anything other than the candidate's
assessment.

Assignment
KPMG is hiring for an AI Builder role. The role is intentionally broad: strong candidates may

come from software engineering, UX, MLOps, consulting, research, operations, product, or self-
taught paths. What matters is the ability to enter an ambiguous enterprise context, identify

where AI can create practical value, build something tangible, and explain tradeoffs clearly.
The Task
Build something you think would evaluate an AI Builder well. We are giving you a starting point,
not a fixed problem: help the hiring team identify strong AI Builder candidates, using the AI
Builder job description as your source material. Part of the task is deciding what “evaluate well”
actually means, then building to your own definition. You choose the direction and the solution
format. If you reframe the problem, tell us why.
Why the format is open
AI Builders join us from engineering, UX, MLOps, consulting, research, operations, product, and
self-taught paths. A single fixed task would advantage one background and penalize the rest,
and would tell us more about prior training than about how a candidate thinks. Leaving the
format open lets you lean into your strengths and show your strongest work in your own
domain. We want to see what you are actually good at, not watch you perform a skill you do not
have. What every strong background shares is clear reasoning under ambiguity and constraint,
and that is what we are looking for across all of them.
What we're really evaluating

We are not hiring you to build hiring tools. This exercise uses “evaluate an AI Builder well”
as a deliberately open canvas, because the AI Builder role itself is open. You will routinely
walk into ambiguous problems with no defined solution and decide what is worth building,
and often what the real problem even is. The topic is the vehicle. What we assess is how

you get from a vague prompt to a concrete, defensible build: how you scope the problem,
what you choose to optimize for, the choices you make, and the ones you deliberately do
not.

What you can build
• A lightweight product, app, workflow, agent, or automation.
• An evaluation process, scoring or interview tool, or simulation.
• A candidate workbench, reviewer workflow, prompt/evaluation framework, or prototype
review process.
• Another practical artifact that helps evaluate AI Builder candidates.
There is no single correct answer. Choose the approach that best shows how you think and
build.

Rules and guardrails
• Spend no more than 3 hours total, including building, preparing your explanation, and
recording the video. There is no extra credit for spending more time on your solution. The
cap is tight on purpose. We want to see what you choose to leave out.
• Use your own tools, accounts, and AI tokens. Free-tier tools are completely fine. We are
assessing judgment, not spend. KPMG will not provide tools, tokens, credentials, data, or
environments for this exercise.
• Use only synthetic, public, or sample data. Do not use confidential or personal data.
• Make your artifact inspectable with the lightest supporting materials needed, such as a
demo link, local files, screenshots, a README, sample outputs, or clear instructions.
• We expect you to use AI tools. This role does not exist without them. But remember: you
can outsource thinking, you cannot outsource understanding. We will ask follow-up
questions, and you will be expected to answer them. You should be able to disclose how
you used AI and which decisions were yours.
What we will look for
• Understanding of the AI Builder role and the hiring challenge.
• Ability to turn ambiguity into a concrete direction, including how you frame the problem.

• A practical, usable artifact grounded in the job context.
• Evidence of builder mindset: making something real, not only describing an idea.
• Communication of assumptions, tradeoffs, risks, and next steps.
• Responsible AI judgment, including fairness, governance, transparency, and candidate
experience.
We are not looking for polish for its own sake. We are looking for evidence that you can think in
systems, build pragmatically, communicate clearly, and balance speed with judgment in a
complex enterprise environment.
“Like many complex organizations, KPMG has competing priorities, a need for risk, security
and appropriate governance and a desire to move faster and be innovative in the way we
operate and deliver to clients - you are expected to consider the needs and sometime
competing priorities of multiple stakeholders when considering how you approach answering
this question”
